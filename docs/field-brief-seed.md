# The Estonian "AI Expert" Market — Competitive Analysis

_Research for the egert-ai fork ("hire me" / offer) page. Compiled July 2026._

## Why this document exists

The current landing page (`egert.ai`-style portfolio) proves Egert **built 45 apps**. It does **not** land the second message: _"…and you can hire this person to build yours."_ Before writing that offer, we mapped everyone in Estonia already selling something adjacent — so the fork page can be positioned **against the whole field**, not in a vacuum.

The working file was titled `EE_AI_Sharlatanid` ("charlatans"). That framing is directionally right but needs precision: **most of these players are not frauds** — they are legitimate trainers and agencies. The opportunity isn't to call them charlatans. It's that **almost none of them can show what Egert can show: a wall of real, live, clickable, production software they built with their own hands.** They sell _talk about_ AI, _teaching_ AI, or _advising on_ AI. Egert sells _shipped product_. That is the entire wedge.

## The five archetypes (and where each is weak)

### A. Corporate AI trainers — _sell teaching_
**Productory, Tehisintellekt.ee, AI Turundus, Kasulik.ai, most of the long tail.**

- **Offer:** workshops, cohorts, "AI for leaders" programs, custom in-house training. You leave knowing how to prompt ChatGPT/Copilot better.
- **Communication:** "Pane tehisintellekt enda heaks tööle" (make AI work for you), "praktilised interaktiivsed õpitoad." Professional, anti-hype, competent.
- **Commercialisation:** corporate training days + retainers. Monetise **volume of humans taught**. Metrics are people-counts: _200+ orgs_ (Productory), _3500+ trained / 5400h / €21M+ profit_ (Kasulik), _6000+ participants_ (Non-Tech).
- **Weakness vs. Egert:** They teach you to fish, slowly. **They never show a single thing they built and shipped.** Their homepage is logos and headcount, not product. A business that just wants _the tool built_ gets a curriculum instead.

### B. AI automation / agent agencies — _sell implementation_
**Amperly, AI Eesti, Agentify.**

- **Offer:** audits → roadmap → build (chatbots, agents, workflow automation). This is the group that actually **builds**, so it's Egert's most direct overlap.
- **Communication:** "kasvupartner" (growth partner), "we don't send an 80-page report" (AI Eesti), "working from day one" (Agentify), "3–10x productivity" (Amperly). Outcome-led.
- **Commercialisation:** free audit/prototype as lead magnet → paid implementation → ongoing support retainer. **Agentify is the sharpest** — explicitly names Claude Code / Codex, positions as builders not trainers, has an "Agentify OS."
- **Weakness vs. Egert:**
  - **Anonymized proof.** "A fintech platform," "a furniture manufacturer," "20+ pages" — nothing you can click. Egert's case studies are **live URLs with real users and payments**.
  - **Agency shape** = overhead, coordination, markup, slower. Egert is **one person, direct, no agency layer**.
  - **Narrow output** — mostly chatbots/automations. Egert ships **full products**: booking systems, PWAs, multi-tenant SaaS, NLP engines, scraping pipelines.

### C. Vibe-coding coaches & community — _sell "you can build too"_
**Kei Olbrei (vaibkood/keivibes), Marika Juusu (vaibkoodimine), UX Estonia, Veebikool.**

- **Offer:** beginner workshops, wine-coding evenings, brunches, festivals. "Build your first app in 2 hours." Empowerment for solopreneurs and non-coders.
- **Communication:** warm, accessible, aspirational — "building my future one prompt at a time," "solo/small team building what used to need an agency."
- **Commercialisation:** event tickets (Fienta/winecode.io), B2B training on request, festival sponsorships (VaibFest 2026).
- **Weakness vs. Egert:** **"Vibe coding" now signals _prototype_.** The output is a first app, a landing page, a demo. Egert is the **antidote to vibe-coding's ceiling** — the person you call when the prototype needs to become a real product with auth, payments, tests and uptime. Different altitude entirely.

### D. The research/evidence lab — _sell credibility_
**Kodulabor.**

- **Offer:** publishes controlled AI experiments on real workflows with honest metrics. Sells nothing directly (yet).
- **Communication:** _"Ei mingit hype'i. Ei mingit müügikõnet. Ainult tõendid."_ (No hype. No sales pitch. Only evidence.) — the **single best-positioned voice in the whole market**.
- **Weakness vs. Egert:** it's a reading room, not a builder-for-hire. But **its tone is Egert's natural register** — Egert should borrow the evidence-first posture and go one step further: _not just measured evidence, but shipped product._

### E. The contrarian — _sell the opposite of AI_
**Human Intellect (Helen Anijalg).**

- Sells nature retreats to "protect human thinking in the age of AI." Not a competitor; useful as proof the market is crowded enough that a counter-niche already exists.

### F. Government — _not commercial_
**eesti.ai** (Riigikantselei national program). Include for ecosystem literacy only; not a rival. (It was likely on the list to understand the landscape, not as a target.)

## What the whole market has in common (every weakness = Egert's opening)

1. **Nobody shows a wall of shipped product.** The entire field proves itself with **logos, headcounts, and testimonials**. Not one leads with _"here are 45 live things I built — click any of them."_ This is Egert's unfair advantage and it is currently under-exploited even on his own page.
2. **Proof is anonymized or abstract.** Agencies say "a client in fintech." Egert says "Saku Tenniseklubi — here's the live booking system, deployed and handed over."
3. **Pricing is hidden — universally.** Every single player says "book a call" / "contact for a quote." A concrete, productised offer (even indicative ranges) would be **radically differentiating** and filter for serious buyers.
4. **Most sell _learning to do it_, not _it being done_.** The market is saturated with training. The under-served buyer is the one who says _"I don't want a course. I want the thing built, this month."_
5. **"AI" is the product, not the outcome.** They sell "AI adoption," "AI readiness," "AI workflows." Egert sells **software that solves a business problem** — the AI is just how it got built fast. That reframing ("I use AI to build your software cheaply and fast" vs. "I sell you AI") is cleaner than anything in the field.
6. **Builder credibility is thin.** Trainers have marketing/HR/strategy backgrounds; agencies are young teams (AI Eesti avg age 23). Egert is a **Product Manager at SK ID Solutions** (digital identity / signing infrastructure — serious production software) **who also personally ships production code**. That PM-who-actually-builds combination doesn't exist elsewhere on this list.

## The one genuinely dangerous competitor

**Agentify** is the only player who overlaps Egert's real territory: Claude-Code-native, builds real products, targets SMEs, free-prototype lead magnet. If Egert doesn't plant a flag, Agentify owns "the people who actually build with Claude Code in Estonia."

**How Egert out-positions Agentify:**
- **Named, clickable, live proof** vs. their anonymized case studies.
- **One accountable person** vs. a studio (no handoffs, no account managers).
- **Breadth of shipped product types** (SaaS, PWA, NLP, booking, scraping, data) vs. their agent/automation focus.
- **A real product-engineering standard** — tests, golden sets, deterministic validation, DST-safe timezones, 33-step Playwright suites. That depth is visible and checkable; an agency's isn't.

## Strategic conclusion (feeds directly into the offer page)

The fork page should make **one argument the entire market cannot answer**:

> **"Estonia has no shortage of people who will teach you AI, audit your AI readiness, or run a wine-coding evening. It has almost no one who will just build you the actual software — production-grade, this month, one accountable person, and show you 45 live things they already shipped to prove it."**

Everything on the fork page — hero, offer packages, proof wall, process, price — should serve that sentence. Detailed execution in `02-fork-page-recommendations.md`.
